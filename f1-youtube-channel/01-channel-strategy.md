# 01 — Channel Strategy

## 1. Positioning

**The gap we fill:** Most F1 content on YouTube is either (a) instant reaction
videos with thin analysis, or (b) slow, high-production documentaries. There's
a sweet spot in the middle: *fast enough to be relevant, thoughtful enough to
be worth the watch.* We win on **clarity and insight per minute**, not on being
first or being flashiest.

**Promise to the viewer:** "Whatever just happened in F1 — or whatever you've
always wondered about it — we'll explain it better than your group chat did."

**Why faceless works here:** F1 audiences care about the *subject*, not a
personality. A consistent voice, strong writing, clean motion graphics, and a
recognisable visual identity carry the brand. Faceless also means the channel
isn't bottlenecked on one person being on camera, and it's easier to bring in
help later without rebranding.

## 2. Target audience

- **Core:** Engaged fans (watch most races, follow the standings, argue about
  strategy). They want depth and a point of view.
- **Secondary:** "Drive to Survive" wave / casual fans who search for *who,
  what, why* after a dramatic weekend. They want clarity and story.
- **Tertiary:** Lapsed/nostalgic fans pulled in by history pieces.

Designing for the core keeps quality high; packaging (titles/thumbnails) is
what reels in the secondary and tertiary groups.

## 3. The four content pillars

You chose all four. The trick is to **balance them on the calendar** so news
keeps you topical while evergreen content compounds. Suggested mix once you're
at a steady cadence (e.g. 3 videos/week):

| Pillar | Share | Role | Shelf life | Example shape |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **News & Recaps** | ~40% | Catch the search/trend wave each weekend | Days | "What actually happened at [GP]: the 3 stories that decided it" |
| **Analysis & Explainers** | ~30% | Defensible, demonstrates the channel's brain | Weeks–months | "Why [team] keeps losing in the pit window" / "The 2026 regs, explained" |
| **Drama & Storytelling** | ~20% | Retention + shareability | Months | "The feud nobody's talking about" / "How [driver]'s season fell apart" |
| **History & Deep Dives** | ~10% | Fully evergreen, lowest footage/legal pressure, builds authority | Years | "The race that changed the rulebook forever" |

**Programming logic:**
- **Race weekends** → News/Recap is the priority (Sun night or Mon morning).
- **Mid-week** → Analysis or Drama that builds off the weekend's themes.
- **Off-weekends / winter break** → History deep dives and evergreen explainers
  (you can batch-produce these in advance to cover gaps).

Cross-pollinate: a single weekend can spawn a recap (news), a "why did the
strategy fail" (analysis), and a "this rivalry just got personal" (drama).

## 4. Naming & branding

**Name criteria:** memorable, not infringing on F1®/Formula 1® trademarks
(don't use "F1", "Formula 1", "Grand Prix", or team/driver names *in the channel
name* — that invites both trademark trouble and channel-name rejections), easy
to say in the voiceover outro, and `.com`/handle available.

**Direction ideas (check availability before committing):**
- *Apex Notes* / *Apex Lines* — racing line + "notes/insight".
- *The Slipstream Brief* — drafting metaphor + briefing.
- *Backmarker to Front* — underdog/analysis tone.
- *Paddock Theory* — analysis/insider tone.
- *Pit Wall Perspective* — strategy/analysis.
- *Turn One Takes* — fast, opinionated.

**Visual identity (faceless-friendly, low-cost to keep consistent):**
- A bold, legible **logo + wordmark** and 2–3 brand colours (avoid F1's
  signature red-on-black if you want to look distinct and avoid implied
  affiliation).
- A reusable **intro sting** (2–3s) and **lower-thirds / stat cards** template.
- A consistent **thumbnail system**: same font, same colour blocking, big
  readable subject + 3–4 word hook. Consistency is what makes the channel
  recognisable in a feed.
- One **voice** (see below) used on every video.

## 5. Voice & tone

- **Tone:** knowledgeable mate, not breathless hype. Confident takes, but
  honest about uncertainty. Dry humour welcome; clickbait-betrayal not.
- **Narration voice:** pick ONE and keep it consistent — it *is* the brand.
  Options, cheapest→best:
  1. High-quality AI TTS (e.g. ElevenLabs-class) with a custom/cloned voice you
     have rights to use. Fast, scalable, good enough at the top tier.
  2. Your own voice (matches "AI-assisted, manual" if you ever switch).
  3. A paid VO artist on retainer (best warmth, higher cost/slower).
- **Script style:** short sentences, strong cold open (hook in first 5s), no
  "hey guys welcome back", signpost structure ("three things…"), end with a
  question or forward-look to drive comments + the next video.

## 6. What "unique and insightful" means in practice

The differentiator isn't *information* (everyone has the result); it's
**framing and synthesis**:
- Connect the weekend to the **season arc** (standings, momentum, regs).
- Bring **one non-obvious angle** per video (a stat, a strategic read, a
  historical parallel).
- Take a **clear position** and defend it — then invite disagreement.
- Always answer the viewer's real question, which is usually *"so what does this
  mean?"* not *"what happened?"*
