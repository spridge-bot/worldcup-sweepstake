# 04 — SEO, Packaging & Growth

On YouTube, **packaging (title + thumbnail) decides whether a video gets a
chance**, and **retention decides whether it spreads**. Great research is
wasted behind a weak thumbnail. Budget real time for both.

## Discovery: how people find F1 content

Two engines, and we feed both:

1. **Search** (evergreen, demand you can predict) — "explained", "why",
   "what happened", "how does … work", driver/team names + question words.
   Analysis, Explainers, and History pieces live here and compound for years.
2. **Browse/Suggested** (the volume game) — driven by title/thumbnail CTR and
   retention. News/Recaps and Drama ride this, especially in the 48h after a
   race when the topic is hot and the algorithm is pushing F1 to fans.

**Implication:** evergreen pieces are your *foundation* (steady, compounding
search traffic); trend pieces are your *spikes* (subscriber bursts after big
weekends). You need both — which is exactly why all four pillars.

## Keyword & topic research (ties into Stage 1–2 of the pipeline)

- Start from **search demand**: autocomplete, "people also ask", keyword/trend
  tools. Post-race, watch for spiking queries (incidents, penalties, "did X
  cheat", "why did Y pit").
- Validate with **YouTube's own search** — what's ranking, how old, how good?
  Look for **high-demand / weak-supply** gaps (lots of searches, only mediocre
  videos). Those are your best shots.
- Map each idea to a **primary keyword** that goes in the title, first line of
  the description, and is spoken early in the script.

## Titles

- **Front-load the hook and the keyword.** First ~40 characters matter most
  (mobile truncation).
- **Curiosity + clarity**, never curiosity *alone* (clickbait betrayal tanks
  retention and trust). The video must deliver the title's promise.
- Patterns that work for F1:
  - "Why [team] is *actually* in trouble" (analysis)
  - "What everyone missed at the [GP]" (recap)
  - "The [driver] problem nobody wants to admit" (drama)
  - "How [rule/tech] *really* works" (explainer)
  - "The race that broke F1" (history)
- A/B test titles after publish if your tooling allows; iterate the
  underperformers.

## Thumbnails

- **A consistent system = brand recognition.** Same font family, same colour
  blocking, same composition logic on every video.
- **One clear subject, 3–4 words max**, huge and legible at phone size.
- High contrast, expressive focal point, no clutter. Test at 10% size.
- For a faceless channel, lean on **bold typography + iconography + your
  graphics** (a sharp standings-swing chart, a track map, a helmet silhouette)
  rather than faces.
- Title and thumbnail should be **complementary, not redundant** (thumbnail
  raises the question, title frames it — or vice versa).

## Description, tags, chapters

- **Description:** primary keyword in the first line; a 2–3 sentence genuine
  summary; relevant secondary keywords naturally; links; consistent boilerplate
  (channel blurb, socials) at the bottom.
- **Chapters:** add timestamps — helps retention navigation and can surface in
  search. Natural fit for "three things…" structure.
- **Tags:** minor ranking factor now, but include the obvious variants.
- **Pinned comment:** pose the debate question to kick off engagement.

## Retention (the real growth lever)

- **Hook in 5 seconds** — state the stakes or the surprising claim before
  anything else. No logo-first 8-second intro.
- **Pace** — tight cuts, motion every few seconds, on-screen text reinforcing
  VO. F1 viewers bounce fast.
- **Open loops** — tease the payoff ("the third reason is the one that actually
  decides the title…") to pull viewers through.
- **Deliver the promise** and end with a **forward-look + question** → comments
  + click to the next video.
- Watch the **audience-retention graph** per video; the dips tell you exactly
  what to cut next time.

## Cadence

- **Consistency > frequency.** Pick a cadence you can sustain through a 24-race
  season *and* the off-weeks.
- Suggested ramp:
  - **Phase 1 (pilot):** 1 video/week, nail the template and the look.
  - **Phase 2:** 2/week (1 trend + 1 evergreen).
  - **Phase 3:** 3/week (recap + analysis + drama/history), using a batched
    evergreen buffer to survive busy weekends.
- **Shorts:** strong top-of-funnel for F1 (a stat, a hot take, a 30s explainer
  clipped from a long video). Use them to feed subscribers to long-form; don't
  let them become the whole channel (lower RPM, weaker loyalty).
- **Race-weekend playbook:** pre-write the recap skeleton before the race; drop
  facts in live; fast-path edit; publish Sunday night / Monday AM while the
  topic is peaking.

## Community & off-platform

- Reply to early comments (signals + insight for next videos).
- Reuse the research into a newsletter / X / TikTok / Reddit presence to
  diversify discovery and own an audience the algorithm can't take away.
- Cross-link videos with end screens and playlists by pillar (binge paths).
