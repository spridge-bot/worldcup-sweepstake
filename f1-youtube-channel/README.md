# Faceless Formula 1 YouTube Channel — Planning Package

A working blueprint for a faceless F1 channel that covers the stories and
questions people are actively searching for, produced through a
**semi-automated, AI-assisted pipeline** with a **compliant-first** approach
to footage.

## The one-paragraph pitch

A faceless F1 channel that turns each race weekend (and the quieter weeks
between) into clear, opinionated, well-sourced video. We listen to what the
wider F1 conversation is saying — across the most-watched videos, news, and
forums — extract the recurring themes and unanswered questions, then publish
our **own** take: tighter, better-structured, and more insightful than the
average reaction video. No on-screen presenter; the value is in the research,
the writing, the voice, and the edit.

## Decisions already made

| Decision | Choice | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Content angle | **All four pillars** — News/Recaps, Analysis/Explainers, Drama/Storytelling, History | A pillar mix smooths the calendar: news rides trends, evergreen pieces compound, drama drives retention. |
| Automation level | **Semi-automated** | AI does research/drafting/voiceover; a human approves every script and final cut. Protects quality and avoids policy strikes. |
| Footage | **Compliant-first** | Build on licensable/stock/official-embed/original-graphics so the channel is monetisation-safe and not one claim away from deletion. |

## How to use it

Start with `01` to settle identity, then `02` to stand up the pipeline and run
one pilot video before committing to a cadence. `03` is non-negotiable reading
before you publish anything with footage in it. Treat every doc as a living
draft — update as you learn what your audience actually rewards.

> **Reality check on legal:** Formula One Management (FOM) is one of the most
> aggressive rights-holders on YouTube. The compliant-first strategy in `03`
> is what keeps this channel alive long-term. Read it before you fall in love
> with the idea of using race clips.
