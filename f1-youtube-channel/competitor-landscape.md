# Competitive Landscape: Top Videos & Faceless/Graphics-Led Channels

Follow-up to `competitor-the-race.md`. Two jobs here: (1) what kinds of videos
actually win in F1, and (2) study the channels whose *format* is closest to
ours — faceless / graphics-led — because they're our truer competitors than
The Race.

> **Data caveat:** YouTube stats-scraper sites (youtubers.me, etc.) blocked
> automated access, so exact per-video view counts couldn't be pulled
> programmatically. Subscriber figures below are from public sources and are
> approximate/as-of-mid-2026. **Action:** manually log live numbers + top-video
> view counts when you sit down to study these channels (5 min each).

## Part 1 — What wins in F1 (the format signal)

- **Strategy breakdowns are a top-performing format** — strategy-focused F1
  videos reportedly averaged ~**1.2M views in 2024**. This is squarely in our
  Analysis pillar and is *graphics-friendly* (no race footage needed to draw a
  pit-window or tyre-deg chart). Strong signal to make strategy a flagship
  series.
- **The official F1 channel is the 800lb gorilla for highlights** (4B+ total
  views; Australian GP highlights ~13M in week one) — confirming we should
  **never compete on highlights/footage**. That lane is owned.
- **Personality/entertainment crossovers spike huge** (e.g. F1's "Passenger
  Princess" series, 263M+ views) — not our lane, but a reminder that *format
  novelty* and *story* travel further than raw analysis alone.
- **Highlights views trended slightly *down* 2024→2025** even as engagement
  stayed strong — audience attention is shifting toward *explanation and
  story*, away from "just show me the race." Tailwind for our positioning.

**Takeaway:** lead with **strategy/analysis explainers** and **story pieces**;
treat them as the growth engine, with news recaps for topicality.

## Part 2 — Faceless / graphics-led channels (our true peers)

| Channel | ~Scale | Faceless? | What they do | What to steal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Chain Bear** (Stuart Taylor) | Mid (Patreon-supported) | **Yes — fully animated**, simple flat-graphic style, voiceover only | "Chain Bear Explains…" — rules, tech, science broken down simply | The **purest proof a faceless animated F1 channel works**. Simple, repeatable graphic style = low cost, high clarity. A named recurring "Explains…" series. |
| **Aldas** | ~180K subs, 62M views | **Largely faceless**, opinion + info, graphics/clips driven | Opinion-driven F1 takes, fast and personality-in-voice | **Voice-as-personality without a face**; strong opinions drive watch-time and comments. Dubbed "the Gordon Ramsay of F1" for tone. |
| **Driver61** (Scott Mansell) | ~1.3M subs | No (presenter) but **heavily infographic/animation-led** | Deep technical explainers, driving technique, innovations | The **gold standard for infographic explainers**; technique of animating a complex idea cleanly. (He's on-camera — we replicate the *graphics*, not the face.) |
| **Kurzgesagt** (not F1) | Huge | **Yes — animated, narrated** | Science explainers | The **template for the whole faceless model**: gorgeous consistent visual system + crystal-clear narration doing all the work. Our north star for *look + clarity*. |
| **The Race** | ~1.3M subs | No (journalists) | News/analysis/podcast, licensed footage | Topic coverage + recurring series + season-arc framing (see prior doc). |

### What the faceless peers prove
1. **It works without a face** — Chain Bear and Aldas are existence proofs in
   *this exact niche*. The bottleneck is clarity + consistency, not a presenter.
2. **A simple, repeatable graphic style beats expensive one-offs.** Chain Bear's
   flat style is cheap to produce and instantly recognisable. Don't over-invest
   in cinematic 3D early — invest in a *consistent system*.
3. **Voice carries the personality.** Aldas shows opinionated narration creates a
   "character" with no face. Our voice/tone choice (`01`) is doing real work.
4. **Named, repeatable series build habit** ("…Explains").

### Where the gaps / opportunities are
- **Chain Bear** = evergreen tech/rules explainers, less on *current-weekend*
  strategy/story. → We can own **timely strategy & story explainers** with the
  same clarity but a faster, trend-responsive cadence.
- **Aldas** = opinion/news, lighter on rigorous data-viz. → We can be **more
  rigorous and graphic-led** while keeping a strong take.
- **Driver61** = deep tech but presenter-led and slower cadence. → We offer the
  **infographic clarity, faceless and more frequent**.
- **Nobody** is cleanly combining: *faceless + strategy/story focus +
  trend-responsive + consistent premium graphic system*. **That's our wedge.**

## Part 3 — Concrete things to copy into our plan

1. **Make "Strategy" a flagship series** (top-performing format + graphics-native
   + zero footage need). Add to `content-idea-bank.md` as a named series.
2. **Adopt a Chain-Bear-grade *simple consistent* graphic system** for v1 — not
   cinematic. Recognisability > production cost. Lock it in the pilot (`05` P0).
3. **Lean into an opinionated voice** (Aldas lesson) within the defamation rails
   in `03`.
4. **Name 2–3 recurring series** (e.g. "Strategy Decoded", "[Rule] Explained",
   "Title Math") for habit-formation, à la Chain Bear / Driver61.
5. **North-star the look on Kurzgesagt**: a visual system + narration clarity
   that makes nobody miss the race clips we can't use.
