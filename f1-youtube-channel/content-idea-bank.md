# Content Idea Bank & Templates

Reusable **video templates** (so production is assembly, not invention) plus a
starter **idea bank** across all four pillars. The trend-based ideas are
*triggers* — patterns to fill in when the season throws up the specifics.

---

## A. The master script template

Use for every video; adjust beat count by length.

```
[COLD OPEN — ≤5s]   The stakes or the surprising claim. No intro, no "hey guys".
[PROMISE — ~10s]    What you'll understand by the end. Name the keyword here.
[STING]             2–3s brand sting (only after the hook has landed).
[BEAT 1]            Claim → evidence (graphic/stat) → "so what".
[BEAT 2]            Claim → evidence → "so what".
[THE ANGLE]         The non-obvious thing only this channel is saying.
[BEAT 3]            Claim → evidence → "so what".
[PAYOFF]            Resolve the open loop / deliver the promise.
[OUTRO — ~15s]      Forward-look + a question for the comments + soft CTA.
```

## B. The synthesis prompt (Stage 2 → 3)

> Paste harvested transcripts/metadata + verified facts; run with a capable
> Claude model. The goal is **original synthesis, never paraphrase**.

```
You are a research editor for an F1 analysis channel.
INPUTS: [transcripts of the top N videos on <topic>], [search/PAA queries],
[verified facts from primary sources].

1. Cluster the coverage into the 3–6 recurring themes. For each, note how well
   it's already covered (saturated / decent / weak).
2. List the questions raised but poorly answered across these videos — ranked by
   how much demand exists vs how badly they're served.
3. Propose 3 ORIGINAL theses for a new video: each must say something the source
   videos did NOT (a stat, a strategic read, a historical parallel, a
   contrarian-but-defensible take). Do not reproduce any source's wording.
4. For the thesis I pick, draft to the master template. Flag every factual claim
   that must be verified against a primary source before publish.
```

Two hard rules baked in: **(1) synthesise across sources into something new;
(2) flag facts for human verification.** See `03` for why rule 1 is existential.

## D. Trigger → video map (post-weekend)

When the weekend produces X, make Y:

- **Controversial penalty/steward call** → explainer: "Were the stewards right?
  The rule, the precedent, the verdict."
- **Strategy blunder** → autopsy: "The pit call that cost [team] the win."
- **Surprise result / breakout drive** → analysis: "Was it real, or a one-off?"
- **Driver/team public spat** → drama: "What the [X]–[Y] row is really about."
  *(Mind the defamation rules in `03`.)*
- **Standings swing** → "The title math just changed — here's how."
- **Reliability/DNF pattern** → analysis: "[Team] has a problem. Here's the data."
- **Regulation news / silly-season move** → explainer + implications.

## E. Evergreen starter list (batch these for the buffer)

These don't depend on current results — produce ahead and bank them:

1. "The modern F1 rulebook, explained in 12 minutes."
2. "How an F1 race is actually won on strategy, not speed."
3. "Why F1 cars are this fast — the physics, simply."
4. "DRS, ERS, tyres: the systems that decide every race."
5. "The greatest comeback drives in F1 history."
6. "The rule changes that reshaped F1 — and why."
7. "How F1 teams actually make money."
8. "The most controversial finishes in F1 history."
9. "What 'porpoising' / [recent tech term] really was, and how it was solved."
10. "The unwritten rules drivers never break (until they do)."
11. "How a pit stop got down to under 2 seconds."
12. "The cars that broke the sport — banned innovations explained."

> Visuals for all of these = original graphics + licensed/CC stills (Green
> tier). No race footage required. That's the point.

| "Why [team] is *actually* in trouble" | Downward trend line, team colours, 2-word overlay |
| "Were the stewards right?" | Split scales-of-justice motif + incident track-map diagram |
| "The race that broke F1" | Dramatic single-word title + era-evoking iconography |

Keep ONE thumbnail system across all of them — consistency is the brand.
